Places in Thailand

Taiwan offers a diverse array of iconic regions, encompassing bustling urban centers, breathtaking natural landscapes, and culturally rich destinations that collectively showcase the island's multifaceted charm, blending modernity, natural beauty, and historical heritage.

Taipei

Taipei, Taiwan's dynamic capital, is a vibrant metropolis blending modernity and tradition. It is home to iconic landmarks like Taipei 101, a bustling night market scene, rich cultural institutions such as the National Palace Museum, and a diverse culinary landscape ranging from street food markets to upscale dining experiences. The city captivates visitors with its lively atmosphere, technological advancements, and a deep-rooted cultural heritage.

Taipei 101

An iconic skyscraper that was once the world's tallest building, featuring an observation deck with panoramic views, upscale shopping, and dining.

Night Market

Vibrant and bustling markets such as Shilin Night Market, offering a plethora of street food, snacks, clothing, and entertainment.

National Palace Museum

Home to an extensive collection of Chinese art and artifacts, including ancient paintings, ceramics, and rare treasures.

Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

An iconic skyscraper that was once the world's tallest building, featuring an observation deck with panoramic views, upscale shopping, and dining.

Ximending

A lively and trendy district known for its shopping, entertainment, and vibrant street culture, often considered Taipei's "Harajuku."

Longshan Temple

One of Taiwan's oldest and most significant temples, known for its ornate architecture and religious rituals.
